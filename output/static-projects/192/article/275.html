Sweet Couscous with Raisins and Almonds

⏱ 10 minutes🔥 10 minutes👥 4 servings servings

Sweet Couscous with Raisins and Almonds is a delightful dish that combines fluffy couscous with the sweetness of raisins and the crunch of almonds. This recipe is not only easy to prepare but also offers a perfect balance of flavors and textures. Ideal as a side dish or a light dessert, it brings a touch of sweetness to any meal and is sure to impress your family and friends.

Why I Love This Recipe

1

It's quick and easy to make, perfect for busy weeknights.

2

The combination of sweet raisins and crunchy almonds is irresistible.

3

It can be served warm or cold, making it versatile for any occasion.

4

The dish is naturally gluten-free and can be made vegan.

Ingredients

Gather these simple ingredients for a delicious sweet couscous.

Using high-quality couscous ensures a light and fluffy texture. Opt for plump raisins and fresh, crunchy almonds for the best flavor. A pinch of cinnamon and a touch of honey elevate the dish, making it fragrant and sweet. Fresh ingredients make all the difference in this delightful recipe.

Instructions

  1. In a saucepan, bring the water to a boil and add a pinch of salt.
  2. Stir in the couscous and cover the pan. Remove from heat and let it sit for 5 minutes.
  3. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the raisins and almonds, sautéing until the almonds are golden.
  4. Fluff the couscous with a fork and mix in the honey, sautéed raisins, and almonds.
  5. Sprinkle with ground cinnamon before serving. Enjoy!

Tips & Tricks

For a fluffier texture, make sure to let the couscous sit covered after cooking. Fluff gently with a fork to separate the grains.

Avoid adding too much water as it can make the couscous sticky. Also, be careful not to overcook the almonds; they should be golden but not burnt.

Serving Suggestions

Serve warm as a side dish or chilled as a light dessert.

Pro Tips

1

Try adding other dried fruits like apricots or cranberries for variety.

2

For extra flavor, toast the almonds before adding them to the dish.

3

Substitute honey with maple syrup for a vegan option.

4

Add a splash of orange juice for a citrusy twist.

FAQs

Q1

Can I freeze? Yes, store in an airtight container for up to 3 months.

Q2

Can I use quinoa instead? Yes, quinoa is a great gluten-free alternative.

Q3

How long does it last? It can be stored in the fridge for up to 5 days.

Q4

Can I add spices? Absolutely! Try adding cardamom or nutmeg for extra flavor.
